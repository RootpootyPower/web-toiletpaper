Attributing for Commentary, Derivative Works and Redistribution

If you choose to make a derivative work based on the original characters or settings present in Toilet Paper, Rootpooty Power! DEMANDS that you disclose that you have no association with Toilet Paper Production Team or Rootpooty Power!. If you wish to redistribute or rebroadcast Toilet Paper in its original form in any way, you must also disclose that you are not associated with the parties above.

Here are some adequate ways to attribute the work:

For web content, attributing a clip or section of the show as reference (embed the links within the text wherever possible, or include them separately)

Attributing the show as a whole, when using it for commentary or criticism:

“Toilet Paper” by Toilet Paper Production Team, a division of Rootpooty Power! is licensed under CC-BY-SA 4.0.

or

“Toilet Paper, Season X Episode X: [episode title]” by Toilet Paper Production Team, a division of Rootpooty Power! is licensed under CC-BY-SA 4.0.

Attributing the text of the website as a source:

“[Page Title], via the Toilet Paper website” by Toilet Paper Production Team, a division of Rootpooty Power! is licensed under CC-BY-SA 4.0.

altenative MLA citation format

Toilet Paper Production Team, a division of Rootpooty Power! “[Page Title].” The Official Toilet Paper Website, [day] [abbreviated month]. [year], [URL of page]. Licensed under CC-BY-SA 4.0.
